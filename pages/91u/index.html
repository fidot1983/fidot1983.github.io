9891U

You probably got to this page from my ad somewhere about selling my Grumman Cheetah.

So, here you go.

Basics

  • N9891U
  • Bought in June 2014
  • 1977 Grumman AA5A, s/no 0291
  • 655.5 SMOH
  • 2619.5 TTAF
  • Hangared. Most of it's life in CA, in TX since 2014
  • Maintained by Gary Vogt 2010-2014
  • Maintained by David Fletcher 2014+

Condition

There are no known to me problems aside from minor stuff I have noted below. It's been maintained by two of the four renown Grumman shops in the country - Gary Vogt and David Fletcher (links above). I have been and am pretty meticulous with keeping her in good shape.

ADs

Pretty much none. Aileron ADs signed off.

Weight & Tanks

  • 19 gallon (38 total) wing tanks
  • 1444 empty
  • 2200 useful

Engine & Prop

  • O320-E2G, 655 and change hours SMOH. Overhauled in 1999.
  • PowerFlow
  • LoPresti Cowl
  • McCauley 1C172BTM73-59 Prop overhauled in 1998 at 1960 hours total airframe time.
  • Last annual's compressions 76, 78, 71, 72
  • Running on Aeroshell 15W50, Blackstone oil analysis clean
  • New spinner installed 2015, painted 2018
  • PlanePower alternator installed 2014
  • Plugs all new REM37BY's since 2014, 2422.89 TTAF
  • Large oil cooler (AeroClassics 8001602) in 2014
  • New fuel and oil hoses same time
  • New Rapco RAP215CC vacuum pump + new hoses in 2016 / 2564.2 TTAF
  • New mags 2341 TTAF, in 2013. Inspected and repaired 2605.7 TTAF, in 2018
  • New battery in 2018

Panel and Controls

  • 6-pack (T&B is a Century 1 Autopilot)
  • CGR-30P Engine Alanyzer + Tach installed 2014 (and here's the story on how I installed it)
  • King KX155 nav/comm + VOR head with glideslope
  • King kX170B nav/comm + VOR head
  • King KT76 (last inspection: June 26, 2018)
  • PS Engineering Audio Panel and Intercom
  • One intercom jack in the back is inop
  • Leather yoke wraps with built in PTT switches - installed 2018

Airframe

  • New wheelpants in 2014 (RV-10 type)
  • New main tires 2536.1 TTAF, in 2016
  • New canopy and windshield in 2016. I killed a buzzard (no prop strike - glanced off of windshield, and went into the canopy).
